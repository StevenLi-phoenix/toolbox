DTMF Tone Generator

Play Dual-Tone Multi-Frequency (touch-tone) signals — the sounds your landline phone made when you dialed a number. Each key is the sum of one row and one column sine wave. Tap a key to hear it, or paste a sequence and dial it like a phone.

DTMF (ITU-T Q.23 / Q.24) replaced rotary pulse dialing in the 1960s — AT&T trademarked it as Touch-Tone. The four extra A–D keys exist in the spec (1633 Hz column) but were dropped from consumer phones; they survive in amateur radio and some military autodialers. Each tone pair was chosen so no row frequency is a harmonic of any column frequency, which lets the receiver tell them apart even on a noisy line. The minimum recommended tone duration is 40 ms, with at least 40 ms of silence between tones.
