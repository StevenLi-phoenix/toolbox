About Leet Speak

"Leet" (or "1337", from "elite") is an alternative alphabet that emerged on early-1980s BBS culture, where users substituted letters with numbers, punctuation, or ASCII art to evade keyword filters and signal in-group status. By the 90s it was a cornerstone of hacker, gaming, and demo-scene culture. Modern leet ranges from light ("h3ll0") to heavy ("|-|3110") and even multi-character forms ("|\|" for N). It's no longer a security technique — it's a stylistic choice and a source of nostalgia, occasionally still useful for obfuscating text in ways that confuse naive automated scanners.
