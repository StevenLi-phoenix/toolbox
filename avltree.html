AVL Tree Visualizer

An AVL tree is a binary search tree that re-balances itself after every insert and delete so the heights of the left and right subtrees of every node differ by at most one. When that invariant breaks, exactly one of four rotation patterns (LL, LR, RL, RR) restores it — and you can watch each one fire live below.

The four rotation cases

Imbalance is named by where the new key landed relative to the unbalanced ancestor's two-edge ladder:

  • LL — heavy on left, inserted into left child's left subtree → one right rotation.
  • RR — heavy on right, inserted into right child's right subtree → one left rotation.
  • LR — heavy on left, inserted into left child's right subtree → left rotation on left child, then right rotation on root.
  • RL — heavy on right, inserted into right child's left subtree → right rotation on right child, then left rotation on root.

Because AVL re-balances eagerly, lookups are guaranteed O(log n) — the height is bounded by roughly 1.44 · log₂(n + 2). Compare this to inserting 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 into a plain BST, which would degenerate to a height-5 linked list; click the worst-case button above to watch AVL keep that flat.
