Update Rules

Lamport scalar clock — one integer per process.

  • Local event on Pi: Li ← Li + 1
  • Send from Pi: increment, attach Li to the message.
  • Receive at Pj with timestamp t: Lj ← max(Lj, t) + 1

Vector clock — one integer per process in every timestamp.

  • Local event on Pi: Vi[i] += 1
  • Send: increment own slot, attach the whole vector.
  • Receive at Pj with vector Vm: ∀k: Vj[k] ← max(Vj[k], Vm[k]) then Vj[j] += 1
  • Happens-before: a → b ⟺ Va[k] ≤ Vb[k] ∀k and Va ≠ Vb. Otherwise a ∥ b (concurrent).
  • Lamport scalars give a total order consistent with causality but cannot detect concurrency. Vector clocks can.
