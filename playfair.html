Playfair Cipher

The Playfair cipher was invented by Charles Wheatstone in 1854 and promoted by Lord Playfair. It encrypts pairs of letters (digraphs) using a 5×5 key square, making it much harder to crack with simple frequency analysis than a single-letter substitution. It was used by British forces during the Boer War and both World Wars.

Rules

  • Same row: each letter is replaced by the one to its right (wrapping).
  • Same column: each letter is replaced by the one below (wrapping).
  • Rectangle: each letter is replaced by the one in its own row in the column of the other letter.
  • Pairs of identical letters are split by inserting a padding letter (default X).
  • Most implementations merge J into I to fit the 25-cell grid.
