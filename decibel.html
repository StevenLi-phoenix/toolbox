1. Ratio ↔ Decibels

A decibel is a logarithmic ratio. Power doubles every +3.01 dB; voltage / sound pressure / current doubles every +6.02 dB.

Formulas

Power ratio: dB = 10 · log10(P₂ / P₁)    Field ratio: dB = 20 · log10(V₂ / V₁) (voltage, current, SPL, particle velocity).

Sum: Ltotal = 10 · log10(Σ 10Lᵢ/10). Combining two uncorrelated levels of 60 dB and 60 dB gives 63.01 dB, not 120.

dBm: L = 10 · log10(P / 1 mW). dBW = dBm − 30. dBV: L = 20 · log10(V / 1 V). dBu: L = 20 · log10(V / 0.7746 V)  (ref = √(1 mW · 600 Ω)).

dBµV: L = 20 · log10(V / 1 µV) — common in RF receiver sensitivity. dBFS: L = 20 · log10(|x| / FS), so a sample of full scale is 0 dBFS and anything above clips.
